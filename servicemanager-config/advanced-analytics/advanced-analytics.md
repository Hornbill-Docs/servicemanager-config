# Advanced Analytics

The advanced analytics module provides a powerful tool for analyzing and visualizing data within the Hornbill platform. It allows users to create custom reports, dashboards, and data visualizations to gain insights into various aspects of their service management processes.

## Before you begin

* To get the most out of the advanced analytics module, it's important to have a basic understanding of data analysis and visualization concepts.
  * [Measures](/esp-config/advanced-analytics/measures)
  * [Widgets](/esp-config/advanced-analytics/widgets)
  * [Dashboards](/esp-config/advanced-analytics/dashboards)
  * [Slideshows](/esp-config/advanced-analytics/slideshows)
* To access the advanced analytics module, users need the following role:
  * **Advanced Analytics User**: This role grants access to the advanced analytics module, allowing users to create and view reports, dashboards, and data visualizations.
* Familiarity with the [Service Manager database schema](/servicemanager-external-db/welcome) will also be beneficial.

## Measures

Measures are the building blocks of your analytics. They represent the data you want to analyze and can be based on various fields and calculations within the Hornbill platform. You can create custom measures to track specific metrics that are important to your organization.

## Widgets

Widgets are the visual components that display your measures. They can be configured to show data in different formats, such as tables, charts, or graphs. Widgets can be added to dashboards and slideshows to create comprehensive reports and presentations.

## Dashboards

Dashboards are collections of widgets that provide an overview of specific metrics or areas of interest. They can be customized to display the most relevant information for different teams or stakeholders within your organization.

## Slideshows

Slideshows are a series of dashboards that can be presented in a sequential manner. They are ideal for sharing insights during meetings or presentations, allowing you to walk your audience through the data in a structured way.
